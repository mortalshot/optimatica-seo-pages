Why You Need a Roofing Permit

Getting a new roof is an expensive investment, so ensuring you have the proper permits is essential. Here are a few reasons why roofing permits are required:

Codes and Safety

Roofing permits ensure your new roof meets building codes for safety and quality. Permit fees also help fund the inspection process. While the fees may seem annoying, they help prevent shoddy workmanship and unsafe conditions that could cost you more.

Insurance Requirements

Most homeowners insurance policies require that any major work like reroofing be done according to local building codes and have the proper permits. If there is damage to your home down the line and permits are not obtained, your insurance company could deny a claim.

Resale Value

Proper permits and inspections become part of the public record for your home. When you sell your house, buyers and realtors will check those records to ensure all work is done legally and safely. Permits help provide peace of mind and can positively impact your home's resale value.

As you can see, roofing permits are more than just following the rules. They help ensure your new roof is safe, high-quality, and won't cause issues with insurance or home sales down the road. While it may require a small upfront investment, getting the right permits for your reroofing project is well worth it in the long run.

Roofing Permit Requirements: What You'll Need

To get your roofing permit, you'll need to gather some important paperwork. Here's what you'll typically need:

Proof of Ownership

Bring documentation proving you own the property, like a deed or title. Some areas may require additional ID.

Roofing Specifications

Provide details on the new roof you plan to install, including:

  • Roofing material (asphalt shingle, metal, tile, etc.)
  • Manufacturer and color
  • Number of layers being removed and installed
  • Roof pitch or slope
  • Plans or Blueprints (if available)

While not always required, plans or blueprints can help expedite the permit process. If you have them, bring copies.

Contractor License and Insurance

If hiring a contractor, ensure they are licensed and insured in your area. Some places require proof of both for the permit.

Permit Application

You'll need to fill out an application for your specific roofing project. Forms are typically available on the local building department's website or in their office.

Permit Fees

There will typically be small permit fees, usually a percentage of the total roofing cost or a flat rate for residential projects. Fees vary in different areas, but expect to pay between $50 to $500.

With the right documentation and details on your roofing plans, obtaining a permit should be straightforward. But if you have any questions, don't hesitate to contact your local building department. They're there to help you through it and ensure your new roof meets all safety codes.

How to Apply for a Roofing Permit

To obtain a roofing permit, you'll need to apply with your local building department. The requirements and costs can vary in different areas, but here are the basic steps to follow:

Gather the Necessary Information

Before applying, make sure you have details about your roofing job, including:

  • The address of the property
  • Property owner information
  • Contractor information (if using a contractor)
  • Roofing materials (asphalt shingles, metal panels, etc.)
  • Total square footage of the roof

Depending on local regulations, you may also need site plans, architectural drawings, and other specifications. It's best to check with your building department to determine their requirements.

Submit an Application

Head to your local building department to submit a roofing permit application, either in person or, in some cases, online or through the mail. You must pay an application fee, typically a few hundred dollars. The application will require information like the property details, owner/contractor contact info, and the type of work being done.

Review and Approval

Once submitted, your application will be reviewed to ensure it meets all building codes and zoning regulations. This usually takes a few business days to a couple of weeks. If approved, you will receive your roofing permit. Some areas provide same-day permit approval for smaller residential roofing jobs. If denied, you will receive a notice explaining why and what needs to be corrected to obtain approval.

Schedule Inspections

Most areas require inspections at certain points of the roof installation to ensure it meets requirements. You will need to schedule a final inspection with your building department once the work is complete. The inspector will check that the new roof is installed adequately before issuing a certificate of completion.

Where to Get Your Roofing Permit

To obtain a roofing permit, you'll need to visit your local building department. This is typically part of your city or county government. You can find their contact information on your municipal website or give them a call. Let them know you need a permit for reroofing or installing a new roof.

In Person

For many homeowners, visiting the building department in person is the easiest way to get your roofing permit. They will have all the necessary forms and applications on hand for you to fill out.

Online

Some municipalities now offer the option to apply for roofing permits on their website. You will still need to provide the same information as applying in person. Uploading diagrams, photos, or roofing specifications may also be required. The building department will review your application and documentation before approving an online permit. Fees for online permits are usually comparable to in-person.

Whether applying in person or online, once your roofing permit is approved and paid for, you are cleared to start your roofing project. Schedule any required inspections to ensure your new roof meets all building codes before final approval. Following the proper permitting process will give you peace of mind, knowing your roof was installed legally and safely.

What a Roofing Permit Costs: Fees and Processing Times

The fees associated with obtaining a roofing permit will vary depending on where you live and the specifics of your roofing job. An average residential reroofing project typically ranges from $50 to $500. The permit fee usually covers the administrative costs of reviewing and approving your application. Some areas may charge additional fees for inspection.

Permit Application Fee

The permit application fee covers the cost of the local building department reviewing your application and plans to make sure they meet the building code requirements for your area. Fees are often calculated based on the value of the construction work so that a full roof replacement will cost more than a minor repair. Expect to pay between $50 to $200 for the permit application.

Inspection Fees

In many places, separate inspection fees are charged for the required inspections during construction, such as a mid-roof inspection and final inspection. Fees for roofing inspections typically range from $50 to $150 per inspection. The specific inspections required will depend on the scope of your roofing project.

Planning and Zoning Fees

Additional fees may apply if your roofing project requires approval from the planning and zoning department. For example, if you are changing the roofline or installing roof equipment like solar panels, the planning department may charge review fees of $50 to $500, depending on the complexity.

The total time required to obtain a roofing permit varies but usually takes between 3 to 14 business days, depending on the workload of your local building department. More complex roofing projects requiring zoning or planning approval may take additional time. The best approach is to apply for your roofing permit at least 2 to 4 weeks before construction to ensure no delays.
