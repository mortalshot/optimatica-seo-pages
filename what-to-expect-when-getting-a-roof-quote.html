Gathering Information for an Accurate Roofing Quote

Getting an accurate roofing quote is vital for your project. Multiple quotes allow you to compare services, materials, and costs to choose a company you trust to do quality work at a fair price.

Here's what to expect when getting a roof quote:

You'll provide information like the square footage, number of stories, and current roofing material. Be as detailed as possible about any damage or leaks. The more info you give, the more accurate the quote.

Ask questions like:

  • What roofing materials do you recommend for my home and the local climate? Asphalt, metal, tile, slate?
  • What brands and types of shingles or tiles will you use?
  • How long is the workmanship warranty? You want at least 2-5 years of coverage.
  • Do you have proper insurance and licensing for this type of work
  • Your roof's at stake, so check their credentials!

Compare quotes carefully based on these factors, not just cost. A low quote may mean poor materials or service. Walk away if something feels off. Trust your instincts—you want a company that will do the job right the first time.

Once you choose a company, get the quote and all promises in writing. Read the fine print so you know what to expect. Feel free to negotiate if needed.

You'll find an experienced roofer offering a fair quote and quality work by asking the right questions. Feel free to ask as many questions as you need. After all, your roof is one of the most critical investments in your home!

When companies provide a roof quote, here's what you can expect:

  • Contact details for the company, including who gave you the quote. Please get to know them!
  • A timeline for how long the project will take. Most residential roofs can be done in 3-5 days.
  • A list of the specific materials and services to be provided. This includes shingles or tiles, underlayment, vents, flashing, and cleanup.
  • The total cost of materials and labor. Get cost breakdowns for each to compare.
  • Payment terms, like a deposit to start and final payment once completed.

Labor Costs: How Much Do Roofers Charge?

The roof replacement or installation labor is a sizable portion of the total cost. Roofers charge for their time, skills, and the physical demands of the work. Several factors determine how much roofers will charge for your project's labor.

Experience and training

More experienced roofers, especially those with certifications or extensive on-the-job training, typically charge higher rates. They work more efficiently and are less likely to make mistakes that lead to leaks or other issues down the road. Roofers with a proven track record of quality work and customer satisfaction can also offer higher prices.

Job complexity

The labor costs will vary depending on the complexity of your roof. For example, replacing an asphalt shingle roof on a simple, one-story house will cost less than re-roofing a multi-level house with numerous peaks and valleys. Installing a metal roof or slate tiles also requires more skill and time, so you can expect to pay more in labor.

Location

Roofing contractors base their rates on the typical wages in your local area. Labor costs are often higher in large cities and affluent communities. Rural and suburban areas typically have lower labor rates.

Additional services

The total labor fees will increase if your project includes additional services like attic insulation, ventilation, skylights, or structural repairs. The roofers must factor in the extra time required to complete the other tasks.

How Much Does a New Roof Cost?

The cost of roofing in the US can vary depending on many factors, including the size of the roof, the materials used, the roof pitch and complexity, the location of the home, and the labor costs in the area. However, the average price of a new roof in the US is around $10,000.

Here is a breakdown of the average cost of roofing in the US by material:

  • Asphalt shingles: $7,000 to $10,000
  • Metal roofing: $10,000 to $20,000
  • Wood shingles: $15,000 to $25,000
  • Clay tiles: $20,000 to $30,000

Additional Fees: Permits, Warranties, and Financing

Getting an accurate roofing quote means understanding all the potential fees involved, not just the cost of materials and labor.

Some additional charges to keep in mind include the following:

Permits

Most areas require permits for roof replacement and repair. Permit fees typically range from $50 to $500, depending on where you live and the scope of your project. Your roofer should handle obtaining the necessary permits, but the fees will likely be passed onto you.

Extended Warranties

A standard manufacturer's warranty will only cover defects in materials, but an extended warranty covers additional years of labor and other potential issues. Extended warranties typically add 10-25% to the total project cost. Consider your roof's lifespan and risk tolerance when deciding if an extended warranty is worth the investment.

Financing Fees

If you need to finance your new roof, there will be additional fees for the loan. Interest rates for roofing projects typically range from 6-18%, depending on your credit score and the repayment terms. Some roofers also charge small administrative fees for setting up financing, usually around $50-$200.

When comparing multiple quotes for your roof replacement, consider the bottom line price and what is included. The cheapest quote may cost more if permits, warranties, and other extras are not accounted for.

Ask each roofer to provide a detailed breakdown of all expected fees so you can make an accurate cost comparison and avoid unexpected charges. Getting a roof replacement is a major investment, so take the time to understand all aspects of each quote before making your final decision.

Look for Red Flags

Be wary of quotes that seem too good to be true. An abnormally low bid could indicate poor workmanship or low-quality materials. Ask questions to make sure you're comparing apples to apples.
