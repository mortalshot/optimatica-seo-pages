Roofing Materials That Withstand Natural Disasters

When disaster strikes, your roof can be your first line of defense for protecting your home. That's why choosing roofing materials designed to withstand high winds, heavy rain, snow, and more is so important.

Metal roofs, like aluminum or steel, are very durable and wind-resistant. They typically last 50-70 years and can handle most weather extremes. Tile roofs, made of clay or concrete, are also impact-resistant and fireproof. They usually last 30-50 years. A metal or tile roof can give you valuable peace of mind if you live in an area prone to wildfires.

For the best protection, opt for roofing materials that withstand at least 90 mph winds. Look for labels like "hurricane-resistant" or "impact-resistant."

Once a professional roofer installs your roof, inspect it at least once a year for any damage or needed repairs. Clear your gutters and downspouts regularly so rain and snowmelt can drain properly.

If disaster strikes and your roof sustains damage, act quickly. Contact your insurance provider to report any claims, then hire a professional roofer near you to make emergency repairs. Temporary fixes may be needed before permanent work can begin. Your roofing contractor can inspect for structural damage and ensure your home remains safe and secure until full repairs or replacement can happen.

The bottom line is that no one can predict when the next big storm, flood, or wildfire may hit. But with the right disaster-proof roof and by staying on top of maintenance, you can feel confident knowing your home and family have the protection they need. Take steps now to prepare for the unexpected - your future self will thank you.

Tornado-Resistant and Hail-Proof Roofing: Impact-Resistant Shingles

Impact-resistant shingles are your best defense when protecting your home from tornadoes and hail. These fiberglass-reinforced asphalt shingles are designed to withstand impacts from airborne debris without damage. They have a tough, durable surface and special sealant that helps them resist tearing. Impact-resistant shingles may cost slightly more upfront but can save you thousands in potential repair costs after a storm.

Popular brands like GAF Timberline HDZ, Owens Corning Duration Storm Impact Resistant, and Atlas Pinnacle Pristine can protect your roof for over 30 years.

Additional Fasteners

Standard roofing nails or staples may not hold shingles in place during tornado-force winds. For the strongest hold, an experienced roofing contractor chooses ring shank nails, spiral shank nails, or steel staples and increases the number beyond the minimum required. These provide more surface area to grip shingles and the roof deck.

Roof Straps or Clips

Metal straps, known as batten strips or roof clips, physically clamp shingles to the roof deck for unparalleled wind resistance. The straps distribute wind forces over a larger area, making shingles less likely to lift or tear off. Roof straps can be installed when shingles are first laid or retrofitted to an existing roof. Either way, they offer an easy way to strengthen your roof and reduce damage from high winds.

Maintaining Impact-Resistant Shingles

To get the most protection from your hail-proof shingles, you need to properly maintain them:

  • Inspect them regularly for signs of damage like dents, cracks, or exposed felt underlayment, and promptly repair or replace damaged shingles.
  • Clean your gutters and downspouts to prevent ice dams and ensure water flows freely off the roof.
  • Re-seal vents, skylights, or protrusions through the roof to prevent leaks.
  • Consider re-coating the shingles with an elastomeric roof coating for added protection.
