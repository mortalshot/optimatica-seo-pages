Regular Roof Inspections

Regular roof inspections and cleanings are crucial for ensuring the longevity of your roof. It's highly recommended to have your roof inspected by a professional annually. These experts have the knowledge and expertise to identify any hidden damage or leaks that may go unnoticed. By addressing minor roofing issues promptly, you can prevent them from escalating into more significant problems. Additionally, professional roofers can assess the remaining lifespan of your roof and advise you on whether it's time to consider a replacement. Trusting the professionals with your roof maintenance will ultimately save you time, money, and headaches in the long run.

Clear Gutters and Downspouts to Prevent Water Damage

One of the best ways to protect your roof and extend its lifespan is to keep rainwater and snowmelt flowing freely through your gutters and downspouts. Clogged gutters can lead to water damage, leaks, and roof rot.

Make it a point to clean out your gutters at least twice a year, in the spring and fall. Use a garden hose to flush out leaves and debris. Or, for a more thorough cleaning, unscrew the brackets holding the gutters to the house and clear them out on the ground. Check those downspouts are also clear by running water through them.

While you're up there, quickly inspect your roof for any visible damage or leaks. Look for curling or missing shingles, punctures, or water stains on the attic ceiling below.

A typical asphalt shingle roof should last 15-25 years with regular gutter cleaning, inspections, and minor maintenance. For the best protection, consider applying a roof sealant or coating, especially if you live in an area with harsh weather. Professionals can properly clean and coat your roof, helping to block UV rays and prevent water damage for many extra years of service.

Put in the effort to keep your roof in good shape. Your attic, ceilings, and the rest of your home will thank you for it! Preventative measures now will save you from headaches—and high costs—down the road.

Trim Overhanging Tree Branches

Overhanging tree branches can cause damage to your roof in several ways. As the branches rub against your shingles or gutters, they can scratch and scrape the surfaces. The branches also drop leaves and debris onto your roof, clogging gutters and downspouts and preventing proper water drainage.

One of the best ways to prevent damage from overhanging trees is to trim branches that extend over your roof regularly. Use pruning shears, loppers, or a saw to cut branches back to the tree trunk. For larger branches, hiring an arborist or tree trimming professional may be best to prune the tree. They have the equipment and experience to cut branches efficiently without harming the tree.

When trimming branches:

  • Focus on cutting branches that directly overhang the roof surface and gutters. Branches that overhang several feet above the roof typically do not pose an immediate threat.
  • Trim branches back to the trunk or a lateral branch, making a clean cut. Do not leave stubby branches that can continue dropping debris.
  • Consider the health and shape of the tree when pruning. Remove up to 25% of the tree's branches at one time, as this can put undue stress on the tree.
  • For fast-growing trees like maple, elm, or oak trees, annual or biannual pruning and trimming may be required to keep overhanging branches at bay. Slower-growing trees may only need trimming every 2-5 years.
  • Hire an expert to trim extra-tall trees or branches that require a chainsaw. Attempting to operate a chainsaw on an unstable ladder is extremely dangerous if you are inexperienced.

By making tree trimming and branch pruning a regular part of your roof maintenance, you can add years of life to your roof and avoid costly damage from falling branches. Your homeowner's insurance company will also likely appreciate your proactive efforts to mitigate risks like fire, water damage, or injury from dead or unstable tree limbs.

Repair or Replace Damaged or Missing Shingles

If you notice any damaged or loose shingles, it's best to have them repaired or replaced by a professional roofing contractor near you as soon as possible. Leaving damaged shingles unaddressed can lead to leaks, mold growth, and expensive water damage to your attic and ceilings. Replacing a few shingles is much more affordable than major roof repairs down the road.

When shingles are repaired or replaced, the contractor will remove the damaged shingles and secure new ones that match your existing roof. They will also check for any underlying water damage or leaks and repair sheathing or flashing before installing the new shingles. It's a good rule of thumb to have your roof inspected by a pro every few years to help maximize its lifespan.

Seal and Protect

Applying a protective sealant or coating to your roof is an easy way to shield it from weathering and environmental damage. Roof sealants typically last 3-5 years before needing reapplication. They protect your roof from moisture, UV radiation, and temperature extremes that can cause deterioration.

Prompt Repairs

Be sure to make necessary repairs. Address any issues like leaks, tears, holes, or damage to flashing around chimneys, skylights, and vent pipes immediately. Minor problems can quickly turn into big, expensive repairs if left unfixed. Hire a professional roofing contractor to handle any repairs or patching needed for the best results. They have the proper training, tools, and materials to do the job correctly.
