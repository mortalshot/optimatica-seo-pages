Have you noticed any dark spots on your roof lately? Before dismissing them as dirt or debris, take a closer look. Those spots could actually be mold, and it's important to identify them as soon as possible. Mold growth on roofs can spread quickly and cause damage if left untreated. The good news is that mold is easy to spot if you know what to look for. This guide will walk you through the common signs of mold on roofs so you can determine if it's time to call in a professional to assess and remove it. Your roof protects your home and everything in it, so keeping it mold-free should be a top priority. Read on to become an expert at spotting those sneaky mold spots!

Inspect the visible parts of your roof

If you're comfortable doing so safely, inspect the roof up close. Look for musty smells, which can indicate mold. Check for spots that feel soft or spongy. Minor dips, cracks, or holes in the roofing material provide access points for water and increase the risk of mold.

Dark streaks along the underside of the roof are also a sign of a leak and potential mold hazard. It's not worth risking your safety for a DIY mold removal, so call a professional roofer if you spot these warning signs. They have the proper safety equipment and experience to fully assess the situation.

Look for Surface Growth and Fuzzy Texture

If you suspect mold growing on your roof, inspecting the surface is the best way to confirm. Look for darker, fuzzy, or slimy spots in texture and circular or irregular in shape.

Surface Staining

Dark stains on your roof, especially along the edges, joints, and flashing, usually indicate mold or mildew growth underneath. The stains indicate excess moisture that feeds the mold colonies.

  • Check where water is likely to pool, like valleys, gutters, vents, and skylights.
  • See if the stains can be wiped away. If not, it's probably mold.

Spongy or Warped Areas

Tap areas of your roof with a rubber mallet. If the surface sounds hollow or spongy, or the shingles are warped or buckled, it likely means water damage and mold growth.

Visible Mold Growth

The most obvious sign is spotting the mold itself. Look for dark, furry, or slimy patches, especially in damp areas. The mold may be grayish-green or black.

  • Inspect the roof carefully, as the surface will be slippery and hazardous. Consider hiring a professional roofer to assess and remove the mold.

It's critical to identify and remediate mold on your roof to avoid serious damage to your home's structure and indoor air quality. Roof mold is notoriously difficult to eliminate due to the height and pitch. Still, a professional roofer has the proper safety equipment, cleaning solutions, and experience to thoroughly remove the mold and repair any water damage to prevent it from coming back.

Be Wary of Musty Odors

If you start noticing musty smells around the exterior of your home, especially in the attic or upper floors, it could indicate mold growth on your roof. Mold gives off a damp, earthy odor as it digests organic materials like wood or drywall. Trust your nose—if it smells musty, chances are there's mold present.

  • Check the underside of your roof for visible mold spots. Mold often appears as black, white, or green splotches or thread-like growths. Shine a flashlight into your attic or crawlspace for a better view. Look for water damage, like warped or sagging areas where leaks may have occurred.
  • Inspect the roof shingles or flashing for cracks, tears, or holes where water seeps in. Water intrusion is the leading cause of mold in roofs. The musty smell is often the first sign before the mold becomes visible.
  • Feel for soft, spongy spots in the wood, especially around vents, skylights, or the chimney. Press into these areas—if your finger goes through easily, the wood is rotted and mold-infested.
  • Go outside and evaluate your gutters and downspouts. Clogged gutters are unable to properly divert rain and snowmelt, allowing water to pool on the roof. Clear out any debris to keep water flowing freely.
  • Consider hiring a professional roofer to fully assess the condition of your roof. They have the proper safety equipment and experience to spot mold that may not be visible inside the attic. Early detection of roof leaks and mold growth is key to minimizing damage and avoiding costly repairs.

A musty smell in the home is usually a red flag, indicating excess moisture and mold somewhere in the structure. Don't ignore the warning signs—immediately determine if mold is present in your roof before the problem escalates. Your roof protects the rest of your house, so it should be a top priority to keep it leak-free and mold-free.

Roof Age and Condition

The older and more worn your roof becomes, the higher the chance of mold growth. Roofs past their useful life of 15-30 years are more prone to leaks, damage, and moisture buildup. It's a good idea to have a professional roofer evaluate the condition and age of your roof. They can properly assess if it's time for replacement before mold becomes an issue.

It's best to hire a professional roofer or mold removal contractor to confirm if you have mold on or underneath your roof. Identifying the Source: A roofer can determine what's causing the mold growth in the first place. There could be a small leak you didn't notice, poor attic ventilation, or moisture buildup under the shingles or flashing. They have the knowledge to spot issues you might miss and recommend solutions to prevent the mold from coming back.

Extensive Cleaning and Repairs: Professional mold removal involves thoroughly cleaning the mold off surfaces, killing any remaining spores, and fixing or replacing damaged materials. Roofers have heavy-duty detergents, tools, and replacement materials to properly eliminate mold infestations and repair or replace roof parts if needed.

Liability Concerns: If you try to remove mold yourself and don't do it properly, any health or property issues that arise could become your liability. A certified roofer will guarantee their work and eliminate mold entirely. Hence, you avoid any liability claims or issues down the road.
