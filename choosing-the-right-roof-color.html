Ready to get started? Let's dive in and learn all about choosing the right roof color!

Popular Asphalt Shingle Colors: Style and Resale Value Considerations

Asphalt shingles are a popular roofing option, thanks in part to the wide range of colors they come in. There's something for everyone, from classic shades like black and gray to bolder tones like blue and green.

Style is usually the primary consideration when picking the right color for asphalt shingles. Black asphalt shingles are a great way to make your roof look sleek and modern, while lighter colors can bring a cozy, traditional feel. Consider your home's exterior color scheme before settling on one—it may be worth investing in some color swatches to see what it'll look like before you commit.

Resale value is also essential when choosing the right roof color for your asphalt shingle roof. While there's no hard and fast rule about the perfect roof color for maximum ROI, darker colors are more sought-after thanks to their timeless appeal. In contrast, lighter-colored roofs require more upkeep than buyers are willing to commit to.

Popular Metal Roof Colors: Long-Lasting Beauty

Metal roofs are a popular choice for their durability and longevity. The most popular colors for metal roofs are Earthy Browns, Grays, Charcoal, and Black. Charcoal shingles provide a soft contrast with traditional white façades, while darker shades like black add an elegant "architectural lace" look to any home exterior. Earthy brown tones are classic colors that look great on any house style, while gray conveys a modern feel that will catch any neighborhood's eye.

Roof Color Regulations in the US: What You Need to Know

When choosing the right roof color, you need to be aware of some regulations in the US. Depending on where you live, local ordinances might stipulate what colors can and can't be used for your roof. For instance, some codes require that all roofs in a neighborhood must have similar colors, so you'll want to check with your homeowner's association and local building code office before you decide on a color and style for your roof.

Aside from local regulations, there are other considerations to keep in mind when choosing the right roof color:

Best Roof Colors for Reflecting UV Rays and Reducing Energy Costs

If you're looking to cut down energy costs, choosing the right roof color is key. Darker roofs absorb more heat from the sun, while lighter roofs reflect it back. If you live in a sunny climate, you may opt for a lighter-colored roof to help reduce your energy costs and keep your home cooler during hot summer days.

Asphalt Shingles

Regarding asphalt shingles, some of the best colors for reflecting UV rays include white, beige, light gray, and shades of green or blue. Some darker colors to avoid include black, dark brown, and slate.

Metal Roofs

Likewise, when it comes to metal roofs, lighter colors are also better at reflecting UV rays and keeping your home cool. Popular metal roof colors for reflecting UV rays include white, cream, and light gray. Some blues or greens could also work well if you want something bolder. Darker colors, such as black or brown, should generally be avoided as they can absorb too much heat from the sun.

Adding UV ray reflective properties can improve energy efficiency and extend the lifespan of metal and asphalt shingle roofs. Different methods and technologies are available for this, including cool roof coatings. These coatings have reflective pigments that deflect sunlight and reduce heat absorption, enhancing UV rays' reflective properties. Cool roof coatings, including lighter shades, come in various colors for greater reflectivity. They are typically made of acrylic, silicone, or elastomeric materials.

Best Roof Colors for Cold Climates: Staying Warm Without High Heating Bills

In cold climates, you'll want a roof color that maximizes heat absorption during the day and minimizes heat loss at night. Dark colors such as black, dark brown, and green absorb more thermal energy than light colors such as white and beige.

In terms of materials, asphalt shingles are the most popular choice for cold climates because they are relatively inexpensive and easy to install. Because of their dark color, asphalt shingles have a higher solar reflectance index (SRI) rate, increasing thermal energy absorption. Metal roofs are another great option for cold climates because they also have a high SRI rate and can be coated with specialized coatings, increasing their thermal reflectivity even more.

So if you're looking for a roof color that will keep your home warm without skyrocketing heating bills, consider darker hues like black asphalt shingle roofs or metal roofs with specialized coatings.
